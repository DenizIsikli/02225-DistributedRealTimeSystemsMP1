This document describes the Mini-project 1 requirements for the 02225 Distributed Real-Time
Systems (DRTS) course. The objective is to analyze and compare the schedulability and response
times of periodic task sets (running on a single core) using different real-time scheduling algorithms.
1 Project Requirements
The students are required to develop their own software to perform the project activities. Any pro-
gramming language and libraries may be used for the implementation. Guidance on the use of Gen-
erative AI is provided under “Content/Course information”.
The project requirements are defined at a high level:
• Determine the worst-case response times (WCRTs) of a set of periodic real-time tasks. The
analysis must consider:
– Deadline Monotonic (DM) scheduling.
– Earliest Deadline First (EDF) scheduling.
– Note: we consider the case when deadlines are smaller or equal to the periods.
• Compare Deadline Monotonic (or Rate Monotonic) with EDF.
• Compare the calculated WCRTs with response times observed during simulations.
The students have the freedom to decide the comparison methodology between RM/DM and EDF,
and the approach for comparing simulation results with worst-case behavior.
